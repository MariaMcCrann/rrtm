The RRTM model simulates the flow of energy, both sunlight and Earth's radiation, in and out of the Earth. The arrows represent the upward and downward flux as a function of altitude, where the width is the quantity of the flux. The orange arrows represent shortwave radiation (sunlight, direct or reflected), the red arrows represent long wave radiation (given off by the Earth and the atmosphere), and the purple arrows represent the total energy flux. The difference in the total flux at the top of the atmosphere is given by the sentence above the arrows.

To control the properties of the sun, surface, and atmosphere, use the control panel on the left. For some atmospheric properties, you need to check the box next to the property to see the profile. To move the profile, drag the red circle at the bottom. The properties you specify are assumed to hold for the entire Earth, and the energy fluxes given are the average taken over the whole surface (including the daytime and nighttime sides of the planet).
